# Audience-world research — recognition before persuasion

> The content must not only say "here is the service." It must say **"I understand the world this
> service belongs inside."** Demo marketing gets attention; cultural fluency builds recognition,
> and familiarity builds trust. Every episode declares its audience and loads the matching
> `_audiences/<name>.yaml` before a single scene is written.

## The three-layer content stack (every piece, no exceptions)
1. **Product proof** — what did you build? (captures, code, evidence)
2. **Context recognition** — what real situation does this belong to? (a scene they've sat in)
3. **Cultural fluency** — what does this audience care about beneath the task? (time, discretion,
   provenance, continuity — not features)

Weak: "NodeRoom lets multiple users and agents collaborate on a spreadsheet."
Fluent: "A private investment team is reviewing a founder before Monday's IC meeting. One person
updates operating metrics, another drafts the memo, an agent pulls evidence from prior notes.
The risk isn't whether AI can write cells — it's whether the team can trust who changed what,
why, and from which source."

## Lexicon — words that create recognition (used naturally, never as cosplay)
**Use:** principal · advisor · investment committee · diligence memo · capital call · family
governance · liquidity event · private markets · portfolio review · philanthropic mandate ·
reputation risk · approval workflow · audit trail · provenance · discretion · continuity.
**Avoid:** elite · premium · exclusive · VIP · "disrupting wealth" · luxury AI · watches/yachts/
jets imagery. *Aesthetic = quiet competence, not loud wealth* — the luxury texture lives in
pacing, typography, calm, and restraint; the substance stays operational.

## The scenes they actually recognize
"Before the meeting, I need the one-page version." · "Who touched this model?" · "Where did this
number come from?" · "Can this be shared with the family, or is it advisor-only?" · "What changed
since last week?" · "Can the next generation understand the thesis without reading 80 pages?"

## The trust-building ladder (content moves people DOWN it, one rung per touch)
Recognition → Competence → Taste → Proof → Discretion → Conversation ("I'd take a meeting").

## The content formula
specific high-trust scenario + hidden coordination pain + cultural stakes + visual proof +
restrained technical explanation + clear decision value.

## Positioning lines (NodeRoom, this lane)
- "A private collaboration room for high-trust work: teams, advisors, and AI agents work on the
  same research, spreadsheet, memo, and decision record while preserving provenance, review, and
  control."
- For wealth audiences: "NodeRoom turns scattered advisor notes, research, spreadsheets, and
  meeting context into a shared decision room where every AI-assisted update remains reviewable,
  source-linked, and human-controlled."
